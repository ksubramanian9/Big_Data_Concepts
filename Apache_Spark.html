The Genesis of Spark

This section traces Spark's journey from a university research project to a global standard in big data. The timeline below highlights the key milestones that marked its evolution, showcasing how a paradigm shift from disk-based to in-memory processing addressed the critical limitations of its predecessors.

2009: The Beginning

Spark begins as a research project at UC Berkeley's AMPLab, led by Matei Zaharia, to address the inefficiencies of Hadoop MapReduce for iterative algorithms.

2010: Open Sourced

The project is open-sourced under a BSD license, quickly attracting interest from the developer community and setting the stage for rapid growth.

2013: Apache Incubation

Spark is donated to the Apache Software Foundation, entering incubation and gaining significant momentum and contributions from a wider community.

2014: Top-Level Project

Spark graduates to become a Top-Level Apache Project, cementing its status as a major player in the big data ecosystem.
